Okay, quick demo of ZeroToShip as the daily signal engine, and OpenClaw as the execution layer on top.

Every morning, a cron job pulls real pain points from Reddit, Hacker News, and GitHub, then clusters them, scores them, and generates ranked startup briefs.

On the dashboard, these are today’s top briefs.

Now open a brief. The key is that the analysis is structured into tabs.

Problem: pain, audience, existing solutions, and the market gaps.

Solution: the proposed approach, key features, and MVP scope. This is perfect prompt substrate for an OpenClaw agent workflow.

Tech spec: stack, architecture, and effort. This is what you feed into agents that generate a repo, a deploy plan, and integrations.

And sources: the evidence links you can verify, and re-use as citations in outbound and landing pages.

Now the operational side. The same pipeline runs daily at 5 AM Pacific, and you can trigger it manually. It runs in graph mode with guardrails: bounded attempts, section-aware retries, and budget caps.

Run history is auditable. Inside a run, the LangGraph workflow trace is the proof: each attempt, the model used, which sections were retried, and why.

And here’s the OpenClaw seam: a handoff step where we call an external agent workflow, keep it bounded with timeouts and circuit breakers, and bring structured outputs back into the brief.

The pitch: hook OpenClaw into the daily cron, auto-run the top one to three briefs, and monitor execution in the workflow trace.

Revenue is subscription for daily briefs, plus paid execution runs per kit.
